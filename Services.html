Fees & Practical Details

I aim to be transparent and straightforward about fees, so you know what to expect from the outset. If you have any questions or concerns around cost, please feel free to ask!

Free Initial Telephone Consultation (15–30 minutes)

This is an informal conversation to help us both get a sense of whether working together feels right. We can talk about what’s brought you to therapy, what you might be hoping for, and whether my approach feels like a good fit. There’s no obligation to continue.

Ongoing Sessions

Online Counselling — £50 per 50-minute session

Sessions take place online or by phone, allowing you to attend therapy from the comfort and privacy of your own space. Some clients appreciate the flexibility this offers, particularly around health, access needs, or busy schedules.

Shorter Sessions

30-minute session (“therapy to go”) — £25

This option may suit you if setting aside a full hour feels difficult due to time constraints, energy levels, or disability. These sessions can offer focused support, though they may feel less spacious than a standard 50-minute session.

Session Length & Commitment

There’s no fixed expectation around how long therapy should last. Some people come for a brief period; others choose to work together for longer. We’ll review this collaboratively, responding to what feels most useful for you rather than following a preset structure.

Concessions

If cost is a barrier, please feel free to raise this — I aim to be as open and flexible as possible. A concession rate of £20 per month is available for clients who cannot afford the standard rate.

What is therapy with me actually like?

Our work usually begins with a short, free telephone consultation. This is a chance for you to ask questions, share what’s brought you to therapy and get a sense of how I work, and whether working together feels right, with no obligation to continue.

Sessions typically start weekly, though this isn’t fixed. Rather than setting a set number of sessions in advance, we’ll review things as we go and adapt the pace or frequency in response to what feels most helpful for you.
